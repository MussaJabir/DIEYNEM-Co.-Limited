## 1. Executive Summary

DIEYNEM Co. Limited does not need a "normal company website." It needs a **digital credibility platform** — an online company profile that does the heavy lifting the printed PDF currently tries to do, but faster, cleaner, and more persuasively.

The reason is simple: DIEYNEM sells to a very specific buyer. Its clients are **government ministries, municipal councils, universities, parastatals (TBS, REA, Watumishi Housing), international main contractors (Beijing Construction Engineering Group, China Wuyi, CATIC, Atkins), and procurement officers** running competitive tenders. These buyers do not make impulse decisions. They evaluate contractors on **proof**: registration class, completion certificates, past contract values, technical capacity, and compliance documents. Today, that proof sits locked inside a 30 MB PDF that mixes a polished profile with raw scanned stamps, a stray US-jurisdiction subcontract template, and OCR-garbled tables. It is credible content presented in a way that undersells the company.

The website's job is to convert that buried evidence into a structured, scannable, trust-building experience. Specifically, the site must function simultaneously as four things:

1. **A digital company profile** — replacing the printed PDF in tenders, emails and meetings ("see our website / download our profile").
2. **A project portfolio** — a visual, filterable record of completed and ongoing works across electrical, ICT, fire, HVAC, solar and rural electrification.
3. **A credibility platform** — surfacing the CRB Class One registration, Business License (Electrical Contractors Class I), TIN, VAT, Tax Clearance, OSHA and WCF certificates, plus third-party completion certificates from clients like Atkins, TBS and the University of Dar es Salaam.
4. **A lead-generation tool** — with clear "Request Quotation" and "Invite us to tender" calls-to-action aimed at procurement officers and main contractors.

DIEYNEM's competitive position is strong and the website should be built to exploit it. The company has **~15 years of operation (incorporated 22 March 2011, p.4)**, **international project experience (Rural Electrification in Uganda, p.5)**, **landmark national projects (the 19-floor Pan African Postal Union building in Arusha, the Tanzania Bureau of Standards laboratories, three airports in 2026)**, and **documented single-contract values exceeding TZS 24 billion in equivalent terms (p.6)**. Among Tanzanian competitors reviewed below, most either have no project portfolio at all or a thin one. A well-built portfolio site would immediately place DIEYNEM in the top tier of its visible online competition.

**Bottom line:** Build a portfolio-and-credibility website, not a brochure. Lead with proof, organize around projects and certifications, and design every page to answer one procurement officer's question — *"Can this contractor actually deliver a project like mine, and can they prove it?"*

## 10. Design Direction

DIEYNEM is a serious, technical, government-grade engineering contractor. The design must feel **engineered, precise and trustworthy** — not decorative or "startup-cute."

- **Colour direction:** an industrial-professional palette. A deep **engineering blue or navy** (authority, electrical/utility association) as primary, paired with a **high-voltage accent** — a controlled **safety amber/orange** or **electric yellow** (drawn from the "Safety First" warning-sticker motif on p.50) used sparingly for CTAs and status badges. Neutral greys and clean white for structure. Avoid more than one bright accent.
- **Typography:** a strong, modern **geometric or grotesque sans-serif** (e.g. Inter, Manrope, IBM Plex Sans) for clarity and a technical feel; generous heading weight for authority; comfortable body line-height for the data-heavy project pages. One typeface family, multiple weights — no script or playful fonts.
- **Image usage:** lead with **real project photography** from the PDF (PAPU power room, transformers, airport commissioning, lecture theatres, solar installs). These authentic, on-site engineering photos are the brand's biggest visual asset — clean them up, colour-correct, and present at high resolution. Avoid generic stock photos of unrelated buildings.
- **Icon style:** consistent **line/outline icons** with a technical edge (single weight), used for services, stats and credentials. No cartoonish or filled novelty icons.
- **Layout style:** a disciplined **grid system** with strong sections, clear hierarchy, and generous white space — a "spec-sheet" cleanliness. Card-based grids for services, projects and certificates. Subtle dividers; restrained shadows.
- **Mood / brand feeling:** *competent, established, safety-conscious, nationally significant.* The site should feel like the digital equivalent of a well-run power room — orderly, labelled, and reliable.
- **Motion:** restrained — counter animations on the stats band, gentle fade-ins, hover states on cards. No heavy parallax or flashy transitions.
- **What to avoid:** scanned-PDF aesthetics; clip-art; rainbow gradients; over-animation; crowded text walls; low-resolution stamp images on display surfaces; anything that reads as "small local handyman" rather than "Class One contractor on national infrastructure."

> **Net effect target:** a visitor's first impression should be *"this is a substantial, credentialed engineering firm,"* within five seconds and without reading a word.

## 13. Admin Panel Recommendation

**Yes — DIEYNEM needs an admin panel (CMS).** The decisive reason: the portfolio is *living*. Ongoing airports will complete, new tenders will be won, the Tax Clearance and Business License expire annually, and project photos arrive continuously. Hard-coding all this would make the site stale within months and dependent on a developer for every update. A lightweight admin panel lets DIEYNEM keep the site current in-house.

Recommended modules (structure and purpose only):

- **Projects** — create/edit/feature completed projects using the case-study template; upload galleries; set sector/service/region/status tags; mark "featured on homepage."
- **Ongoing-project updates** — edit status, progress %, milestones, "last updated" date, and add new site photos for the three airports and future live jobs.
- **Services** — edit service descriptions, capabilities and linked projects.
- **Certificates / Compliance** — replace certificate files, edit numbers and "valid-to" dates, toggle current/expired status (critical for Tax Clearance & Business License renewals).
- **Gallery** — manage the project image library with captions and dates.
- **Downloads** — upload/replace the Company Profile PDF, capability statements and brochures.
- **Inquiries / Leads** — capture and manage "Request Quotation" and "Invite to Tender" submissions; mark status (new / contacted / quoted / won).
- **SEO** — per-page titles, meta descriptions, Open Graph images and slugs (vital for ranking on searches like "electrical contractor Tanzania", "HVAC contractor Dar es Salaam", "rural electrification contractor").
- **Users & Roles** — admin vs editor permissions, so e.g. an office assistant can update ongoing-project photos without touching compliance or settings.

> Keep it lean. This is a content panel for a contractor's staff, not an enterprise ERP — prioritise an obvious, low-training interface over feature breadth.
